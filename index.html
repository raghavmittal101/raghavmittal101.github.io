Multi-modal Intuitive In-situ Scene Authoring in Mixed Reality using GenAI

2024 - 2026 @ TCS Research, IN

This project focuses on developing advanced systems for creating and composing 3D scenes in extended reality (XR) environments using generative AI and multi-modal inputs. The research explores how users can intuitively author 3D content through various input modalities including sketches, audio descriptions, and gestures. I contributed in this project by conceptualising (UI design, end-to-end system architecture) and building prototypes in Unity3D. I also conducted user studies to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed interaction techniques in enhancing user experience and creativity in XR content creation. Publications include GeMR, which won a Best Paper Award at ICVGIP 2025 for its interactive 3D scene composition approach, and Sketch3R, which enables rapid conversion of VR sketches into retrievable 3D shapes. The project also developed SketchTo3DGen, allowing users to create articulation-ready 3D assets from sketches and audio inputs, and InGenCo, which facilitates integrated in-place 3D scene generation and collaboration. The work addresses fundamental challenges in making XR content creation more accessible through natural interaction paradigms, with innovations in sketch-based shape retrieval (FAV3R) that achieve fast and accurate matching between hand-drawn 3D sketches and existing 3D models.

Currently, I am extending this project by building AI agents which can assist users in developing new tools for 3D scene authoring in Unity.

Limitless Dynamic Path Generation For Continuous Locomotion in Virtual Reality

2020 - 2023 @ IIIT Hyderabad, IN

This project addressed the fundamental challenge of enabling continuous locomotion in VR environments within limited physical spaces. This is my Master's thesis project. In this project, I developed a system that dynamically generates limitless paths, allowing users to walk continuously in virtual environments while remaining within constrained physical boundaries. The approach enhances configurability, enabling users to customize path parameters based on their available space and locomotion preferences. I proposed a novel path generation algorithm, built prototypes in Unity3D to run on Meta Quest/2 headsets. I also conducted multiple user studies to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed system. The work resulted in a US patent and two publications that explored both the design and enhancement of limitless path systems. The research demonstrated how dynamic path generation could reduce VR sickness, improve immersion, and provide more natural navigation experiences compared to traditional teleportation-based methods. The system's configurable nature allows adaptation to different room sizes and user preferences, making VR locomotion more accessible and comfortable for extended experiences.

Customizable Head-Mounted Device Development

2021 - 2023 @ IIIT Hyderabad, IN

This project explored hardware innovations in head-mounted display technology for VR applications. The research investigated how hardware transformation in HMDs could enable more effective locomotion experiences, conducting a comprehensive review of existing approaches and their effectiveness. The focus was on development of a customizable, inexpensive 3D-printed HMD specifically designed for detecting eye disorders using virtual reality, demonstrating how VR hardware could be adapted for medical and diagnostic applications beyond entertainment and training.

Requirement Elicitation Methods for VR Products

2022 - 2024 @ IIIT Hyderabad, IN

This project explored how software development practices need to evolve for Virtual Reality, where immersion and spatial interaction change how products are designed and built. I provided my hands-on and current knowledge of VR in this project. By combining systematic studies with hands-on knowledge of the VR ecosystem, this work highlighted why traditional requirement-gathering methods often fall short in VR. The research led to multiple peer-reviewed publications, including a paper in ACM Transactions on Software Engineering and Methodology (TOSEM), one of the top journals in the field.
